Migraine: Evidence-Based Management

Clinical Review Article

By Dr. Nisa Raisani
Consultant Neurologist · International Researcher (NCBI)
January 2025 · 6 min read

Abstract

Migraine is a common neurological disorder causing recurrent headache attacks and significant disability. This article reviews evidence-based diagnosis and management strategies.

Keywords: Migraine, Neurology, Headache Disorders

Introduction

Migraine is characterized by unilateral, pulsating headache often associated with nausea, photophobia, and phonophobia.

Diagnosis

Diagnosis is clinical and based on International Classification of Headache Disorders (ICHD-3) criteria.

Treatment

Treatment includes acute symptomatic therapy, preventive medications, and lifestyle modification tailored to individual patients.
